## SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM

**Headline:** 200,000 Amazon Vine Voices Are Running a Professional Business on Sticky Notes

**Three pain points:**

| Pain | Current "Solution" | Why It Fails |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| Tax liability on "free" products | Spreadsheets + TurboTax | ETV (Estimated Tax Value) is complex, state-specific, nobody explains it |
| Inventory tracking (received → reviewed → resold) | Paper lists, mental notes | Products pile up, donation value unclaimed, missed deductions |
| Review content production | None | 3 hours per video review with no tool support |

**Data:** The average Vine Voice receives $12,000–$40,000 in ETV per year. At 22% effective tax rate = $2,640–$8,800 in unexpected tax bills. Nobody is helping them manage this.

**Visual:** Vine Voice tax bill infographic — "You received $25,000 in products. You owe $5,500 in taxes. Did you know?"

## SLIDE 3 — THE SOLUTION

**Headline:** One Dashboard. Total Business Control.

**Six pillars of Reese Reviews:**

1. **Tax ERP** — Multi-entity ETV tracking, quarterly estimates, IRS form auto-routing (Schedule C, 8829, 4562)
2. **Vine Intelligence** — Live inventory, product lifecycle, received → reviewed → sold/donated
3. **Review Factory** — AI-generated review text + HeyGen video + ElevenLabs voice, two personas
4. **Social OS** — One-click publish to Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn with auto-affiliate links
5. **Financial Hub** — P&L, cash flow, Plaid bank sync, expense categories
6. **FOSS Stream Engine** *(GrowlingEyes)* — Monitor Telegram/Discord/Gitee for incoming products before anyone else knows
