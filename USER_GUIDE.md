## WHAT THIS APP ACTUALLY IS

Think of it as three tools in one:

1. **Your Vine business manager** — tracks every free product Amazon sends you, auto-writes the reviews, and figures out the taxes automatically.

2. **Your tax command center** — keeps track of every income source, every business expense, and tells you what forms you'll need and when to pay estimated taxes.

3. **Your smart financial assistant** — when money moves through your bank account, it notices and says "hey, that heat pump you paid for? You might get $960 back on your taxes."

## THE MAIN FLOWS (How to actually use it day-to-day)

### 🍇 Flow 1: I just received a Vine product

1. Click **Business** tab at the top
2. You're in the Tax Center. Click the **Vine** sub-tab at the top of that section
3. Click **📊 Status Board** (the first tab inside Vine)
4. Click **+ Add Item**
5. Fill in:
   - **Product name** — what Amazon sent you
   - **ASIN** — the product's ID (starts with B, 10 characters, found in the Amazon product URL)
   - **ETV** — the Estimated Tax Value (find this in your Vine account — it's what Amazon will report as your income)
   - **Date received** — when it showed up at your door
6. Click **Add to Queue**

> **Why this matters:** Every Vine item is taxable income. The ETV is what the IRS will say you "earned" even though you got a product, not cash. The app tracks all of this automatically once you enter it.

---

### ✍️ Flow 2: I need to write a review

1. Go to **Business → Vine → 📊 Status Board**
2. Find the item you need to review (red "Needs Review" badge = urgent)
3. Click the item to expand it
4. Click **⚡ Auto-Create Review**
5. The app writes a review for you — title, full review text, and a star rating
6. To edit it: click **Open in Editor →** to go to the full Queue editor
7. In the Queue editor you can:
   - Change the star rating
   - Edit the title and body text
   - Click **📋 Copy for Vine** to copy it to your clipboard
   - Paste it into Amazon Vine
8. Once pasted into Amazon, click **Mark Submitted**

> **Note:** The app writes the review for you, but it won't submit it to Amazon automatically (Amazon doesn't allow that). You have to copy-paste it. The whole copy-paste takes about 30 seconds.

---

### 📦 Flow 3: I have a bunch of products to catch up on

1. Go to **Business → Vine → 📋 Queue** tab
2. Find the **purple toolbar** at the top
3. Click **"Receive & Generate All"** — this marks everything as received AND auto-generates all missing reviews in one click
4. Then go through the Queue and copy each one to Amazon

> **The goal:** 5–8 reviews submitted per day keeps your Vine standing healthy.

---

### 🏦 Flow 4: I connected my bank account (Plaid)

When Plaid is connected (when we're live), every bank purchase gets analyzed. Here's what happens automatically:

1. Go to **Business → Tax Center → Transactions** tab
2. Every purchase shows up here
3. The app has already looked at each one and flagged:
   - 🟢 **"Write-Off"** — This looks like a business expense (Adobe, Dropbox, internet bill, etc.)
   - 💡 **"Tax Credit"** — This might get you money back (heat pump, solar, hearing aids, electric vehicle)
   - 📋 **"Paperwork"** — You should save the receipt for this one

4. For each flagged item, you'll see a **Smart Tax Alert** at the top of the page
5. Click the alert to see what to do:
   - "Classify as Business" → the expense flows into your write-offs automatically
   - "View Credits & Deadlines" → takes you to the tax credit guide

> **Real example:** You pay $3,200 to an HVAC company for a heat pump. The app sees "HVAC Services" in your bank statement and immediately tells you: "You might get $960 back on your taxes. File Form 5695 Part II. Save your invoice."

---

### 🏢 Flow 5: I started a new business or DBA

1. Go to **Business → Tax Center → People** tab
2. Click **"+ Add Business / Company"** button (gold button, top right)
3. The wizard walks you through 4 steps:
   - **Step 1:** Which person owns this business? (or create a new person)
   - **Step 2:** Business name, EIN if you have one, and business type
   - **Step 3:** Which tax form it files on (the app suggests this automatically based on type)
   - **Step 4:** Review and confirm
4. Click **"Add Business"**

> After this, go to **Manage Income** to add any income this business earned.

---

### 💰 Flow 6: Tax time is coming

1. Go to **Business → Tax Center → Deadlines** tab
2. The **red/amber banner at the top** shows what's due in the next 30 days
3. The calendar shows every deadline for the year with:
   - **This Week** (red) = pay/file now
   - **This Month** (amber) = coming up fast
   - **Soon** (blue) = 1–3 months away
4. Scroll down to see **Tax Changes** — what's new for 2025/2026 that affects you, with links to IRS pages to verify

> **Most important dates:** April 15 (file or extension), and the quarterly estimated tax dates (April 15, June 16, Sept 15, Jan 15).

---

### 📊 Flow 7: I want to know what I owe in taxes

1. Go to **Business → Tax Center → Quarterly** tab
2. This shows the 4 quarters of the year
3. Enter your estimated income for each quarter
4. The app calculates:
   - Self-employment tax (15.3% on your net Vine income)
   - Income tax (based on your bracket)
   - Total owed per quarter
5. Mark each quarter "Paid" when you pay via IRS Direct Pay

> **Important for SSDI filers:** You want to pay SE tax because it builds your Social Security wage record. The app explains this in the Deadlines → Credits tab under "SE Tax Strategy."

## THE NUMBERS THAT MATTER

### Your tax situation as a Vine reviewer:
- **Every item you receive is income.** The ETV (Estimated Tax Value) goes on your 1099-NEC.
- **Self-employment tax is 15.3%** of your net Vine income. On $5,000 of Vine ETV, that's ~$765 in SE tax.
- **You can deduct business expenses** — anything ordinary and necessary to run your review business (internet, equipment, home office, shipping, etc.)
- **The SE tax deduction** — you get to deduct 50% of your SE tax from your gross income, which reduces your income tax.

### What to watch for:
| Amount | What to do |
|---|---|
| ETV hits $400+ in a year | You owe SE tax — set aside 15–25% of every item's ETV |
| ETV hits $1,000+ in a year | You probably need to make quarterly estimated payments |
| You install a heat pump | Save the invoice — up to $2,000 tax credit |
| You install solar panels | Save everything — 30% credit, no dollar cap |
| Vine item held 6+ months | Can donate to NoCo Nook as capital contribution |

## IF SOMETHING LOOKS WRONG

1. **Numbers seem off?** Go to Business → People → click the person → Manage Income. Check all income sources are entered correctly.
2. **Missing a Vine item?** Go to Business → Vine → Status Board → + Add Item.
3. **Alert won't go away?** Click the ✕ on the alert to dismiss it.
4. **Need to start over?** Open browser DevTools (F12) → Application → Local Storage → clear all `rr-*` keys. Warning: this deletes everything you've entered.
